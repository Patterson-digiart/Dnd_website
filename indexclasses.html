Class

Fighters, Wizards, Bards, galore!

Your class is one of the most important parts of building a character in DND and whether you're swinging a sword, smiting your foes, or becoming one with nature this guide on Class will be set you on the path to glory!

Choose How You Want To Play

If you want to destroy you enemies with powerful blows but arent too keen on the complexities of magic, pick a martial charcter. If you want the flexibility of magic without the extra rules and strategy to wield necessary to wield it, choose a Half-caster. And if you want to become the spell-slinging magician you were always destined to be and dont care about doing a lot of math, take the plunge and become a Full-caster!

Pick A Subclass

Once you've decided on your class, next youll want to pick your subclass. Subclasses give your character a specific theme to focus on and specific powers that are flavored to where theyve gotten their power and potential plot threads as to why. When choosing your subclass try to keep in mind how that choice will tie into their backstory.

Multiclassing

Everytime your character has leveled up, you have the option to multiclass into another class besides the one youve chosen. Multiclassing is very complicated but it can create unique and powerful class combinations when done correctly, as well as be narratively satisfying as your character changes throughout your story.

Fighter

A Martial class, the fighter does what it says on the tin: fight. Fighters are proficient in almost all weaponry and get multiple attacks at latter levels. You want to hit something choose the fighter.


Barbarian

Barbarians are the rage filled tank class of D&D. They are strong skilled fighters who can take and deal lots of damage. When a barbarian rages they are nigh unbeatable in combat.


Monk

Monks are the martial artist of the D&D world. They spend years training their minds and bodies to work in harmony in order to become powerful foes. Monks are hard to hit and use their spiritual energy, ki, to gain supernatural powers. (This is the class if you want to make an anime character)


Rogue

Rogues are a martial class that specializes in stealth. Rogues are squishy but they make up for that with high stealth and dexterity, making them hard to hit. Rogues are often played as thieves because they can pick locks, disable traps, and do extra damage when they're hidden, but with some creativity they can fit into almost any backstory.


Wizard

Wizards are one of the most challenging and flexible classes in D&D. Wizards have spent years studying magic and the arcane in order to learn how to cast spells and as such they are very intelligent. However because of they spent all theyre time learning like the nerds they are they are also physically weak making them a glass cannon.


Cleric

Clerics are priests and healers who channel the power of their chosen diety in order to cast powerful spells. Clerics are one of the only classes with healing spells but they are also very independently strong and have numerous utility spells making them suitable for just about any role.


Bard

Bards are jacks of all trades masters of none. Bards gain their magic from their artistic talent and sheer charisma and are so versatile they can fit into any niche. Theyre ability to give inspiration to their allies makes them very good support characters.


Druid

Druids are the protectors of the natural world and as such they gain their spells and abilities from nature itself. Druids can command and turn into animals and as one of the most difficult classes to play have one the most expansive spell lists in the game.


Sorcerer

Sorcerers are inherent casters, gaining their magical ability from magic in their blood line. Sorceres originate from many different places (like draconic, wild magic, and the divine) and as such they cast from the power of their own charisma to influence the world.


Artificer

Artificers are scientist who create magical items and potions to give them powers. Artificers are highly intelligent and make magical items that they can use themselves or give to their allies.


Warlock

Warlocks got their magic from making a pact with a powerful extraplanar patron. Warlocks use the power of their charisma in bargaining with their patron to cast up to 4 highly powerful spells and are highly customizable to fit your play style with their various incantations.


Paladin

paladins are holy warriors that fight on behalf of their gods. Paladins take an oath to the gods that they follow an in return they become a strong martial character with the abilityto imbue their strikes with divine energy and smite their foes.


Ranger

Rangers are legolas from Lord of the Rings. They fight with a bow or swords and have nature spells and thats pretty much it. If you still insist on playing one play the updated ranger or any subclass thats not beast master. (Dude just play a fighter with a bow...)


Bloodhunter

Bloodhunters are a secret society of monsterhunters who gain magical powers from Hemomancy or blood magic. Bloodhunters use blood infusions to become that which they wish to destroy and in return take damage to use their abilities. (The bloodhunter is technically hombrew so be careful in official games.)
